Who is Patrick Murphy on Susanna Reid's Death Row: Countdown To Execution and what is death row?

 Posted on July 14, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  586 words  |  Larita Shotwell

CONVICT Patrick Murphy revealed what it's like to be a dead man walking to Susanna Reid in ITV show Death Row: Countdown To Execution.

But what is the real story behind the notorious convict? We take a look...

Who is Patrick Murphy?

On December 13, 2000, Patrick Murphy became notorious when he and six other prisoners broke out of the John B. Connally maximum-security prison in Texas, United States, and they became known as the Texas Seven.

They were Donald Newbury, Randy Ethan Halprin, Larry James Harper, Joseph C. Garcia, Michael Anthony Rodriguez and George Rivas, who was the ringleader.

The group reportedly escaped by leaving in a prison truck with weapons and more than 100 rounds of ammunition.

But on Christmas Eve, a fatal shootout between the escaped convicts and police officer Aubrey Hawkins erupted during a botched robbery - with the latter being shot nearly a dozen times.

To this day it has never been confirmed which convict actually fired the shots - with all seven being held responsible for the cop's death, regardless of their role.

At the time of the breakout, Murphy had been serving a 50-year sentence for aggravated sexual assault.

What happened to the Texas Seven?

The escaped inmates were arrested a month later in Colorado (in January 2001), ending a six-week manhunt.

One of them killed himself as officers closed in and the other six were convicted of killing Hawkins and sentenced to death.

Four have so far been executed, but Patrick and Randy Halprin have not been yet.

Patrick says he was unfairly sentenced in the case concerning the infamous Texas 7 gang.

Murphy has appealed, saying he should be spared because he didn't fire the fatal shots.

Why was Murphy not executed in March 2019?

Murphy, now 57, was due to get a lethal injection at 6pm on March 28, 2019 but narrowly avoided execution after his Buddhist spiritual adviser wasn’t allowed in the chamber with him.

His attorneys had said prison officials’ efforts to prevent the inmate’s spiritual adviser from being with him when he is put to death violated Murphy’s First Amendment right to freedom of religion.

Murphy became a Buddhist almost a decade ago while incarcerated.

Lower courts had rejected Murphy’s argument.

But justice on the court, Brett Kavanaugh, said the Texas prison system allows a Christian or Muslim inmate to have a state-employed Christian or Muslim religious adviser present either in the execution room or in the adjacent viewing room.

His new execution date is yet to be released.

When is Murphy on TV?

GMB's Susanna Reid interviewed Murphy for Susanna Reid's Death Row: Countdown To Execution on June 13 at 9pm on ITV.

She talked to him about life as a dead man walking, as he languished behind bars in Huntsville, US.

What is Death Row?

Death row is a special placement in a prison that houses inmates awaiting execution after being convicted of a capital crime.

The term is also used figuratively to describe the state of awaiting execution ("being on death row"), even in places where no special facility or separate unit for condemned inmates exists.

In the United States, after a person is found guilty of a capital offence in death penalty states, the judge will give the jury the option of imposing a death sentence or life imprisonment without the possibility of parole.

If the jury agrees on death, the defendant will remain on death row during appeal, which may continue for several years.

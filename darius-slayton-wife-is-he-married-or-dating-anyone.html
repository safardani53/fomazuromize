Meet His Parents And Family Members 

Darius Slayton was born into the loving household of his parents, Eddie Slayton and Pamela Slayton.

During the football season, his father, Eddie, stays with him in North Bergen, where he oversees his finances and provides support.

Before each game, Slayton carries a meaningful reminder on his wrist tape: the letters SSC, which stand for Slayton, Stallworth, and Crawford.

These letters pay homage to his grandmother Maxine’s maiden name and hold significant personal importance.

Slayton’s grandfather, Stallworth, despite facing the challenges of dementia in his later years, left a lasting impact on Darius.

He admired his grandfather’s steadfastness, frugality, and even his love for speed. Stallworth’s family has owned 70 acres of farmland in South Alabama since the abolition of slavery, where they raised 14 children.

During a time when Black students did not have access to transportation for their segregated schools, Stallworth’s father took matters into his own hands.

He purchased and repaired an old bus, allowing his children to take turns driving themselves to school.

This upbringing instilled in Slayton the valuable lesson he carries with him: “Work for what you want, and don’t depend on anybody else.”

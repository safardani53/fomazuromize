Helen Maxine Reddy is an Australian American singer, songwriter, musician, actress, and activist. Born in Melbourne, Victoria, to a family of show businesses, Reddy started her career as an artist at the age of 4. She sang on radio and television and won a talent contest on the TV show Bandstand in 1966; Her award was a ticket to New York and a recording audition, but with no success. She continued her career as an international singer, moving to Chicago and then Los Angeles, where she performed her first singles "One Way Ticket" and "I Believe in Music" respectively. in 1968 and 1970. The B-side of the second single, "I Don't Know How to Love Him," reached No. 8 on Canadian magazine RPM's pop charts. She was signed to Capitol Records a year later. During the 1970s, Reddy enjoyed international success, notably in the United States, where she had 15 singles reaching the top 40 of the Billboard Hot 100

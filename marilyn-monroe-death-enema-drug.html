Marilyn Monroe 'killed by a party drug used to heighten sexual pleasure,' PI claims after new witnes

 Posted on June 3, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  759 words  |  Larita Shotwell

A WITNESS has claimed that Marilyn Monroe died from a lethal enema that was commonly used at sex parties attended by the Hollywood starlet, according to a PI.

The then 36-year-old was discovered dead from what was initially determined to be "probable suicide" in her Brentwood apartment on August 5, 1962, hours after she allegedly had an argument with lover Bobby Kennedy.

However, many investigators refuse to believe that she killed herself, and instead have indulged in numerous wild theories surrounding the actress's death.

Conspiracy theorists have placed the blame on the FBI, the Mafia, or her own personal psychiatrist, Dr. Ralph Greenson, who supposedly with a fatal dose of the sedative pentobarbital.

Private Investigator Becky Altringer, who has been studying the case for years, is “100 percent positive” Marilyn didn’t commit suicide, and has now been involved in a German documentary where new evidence came to light. 

She was part of the series Cold Case, which took reviewed events in the episode entitled Tod Einer Ikone [Death of an icon] - Marilyn Monroe.

Altringer says that while filming the episode, she learned that a lifeguard had come forward who attended wild parties hosted by American socialite, Pat, and her husband, British actor Peter Lawford.

Pete, who was part of Sinatra’s notorious “Rat Pack," and his partner, Pat, were known for lavish gatherings at their Malibu mansion in the ‘50s and ‘60s with the likes of the Kennedy brothers in attendance - it's even where they’d allegedly meet for trysts with Marilyn. 

It's rumored that enemas were to be used at these parties to heighten sexual pleasure. 

Now, Altringer tells The U.S. Sun: "I learned while filming a documentary in Germany that there was a lifeguard, who observed several parties at Pat Lawford's house where they had enema parties.

"They were inserting drugs into the enemas so it works faster.

"That’s interesting since there are many theories that Marilyn was murdered [by] enemas."

John W Miner was a former LA deputy district attorney and a major part of the investigation into Marilyn’s death.

Miner was one of the theorists who firmly believed later in life that she died from a lethal enema.

He explained his fatal enema theory in a first-person piece with The LA Times in 2005: "Marilyn Monroe took or was given chloral hydrate to render her unconscious.

"Someone dissolved Nembutal in water by breaking open 30 or more capsules.

"That person then administered the Nembutal loaded solution by enema to Miss Monroe using an ordinary fountain syringe or [an] enema bag. 

"As the drug was slowly absorbed, the tissues of the large intestine reacted to the trauma of exposure to the poisonous substance by an inflammatory response producing congestion and a marked purple color.

"That congestion and purple color were in evidence when the body was opened at the autopsy. Never regaining consciousness, Marilyn Monroe died.

"It must be concluded from the medical evidence alone that Marilyn Monroe was killed by [the] person(s) unknown.”

Altringer additionally told The Sun in 2020 that she discovered a "Box 39" in the special collections section of UCLA library, which is sealed to the public until 2039, and may hold the answers as to how and why the acting legend died.

The box belonged to Greenson and contains various documents and letters relating to Marilyn. 

'UNANSWERED QUESTIONS'

“There [are] so many unanswered questions and there shouldn't be,” said Altringer.

“Marilyn Monroe was the only person whose organs and tests and everything that had been with her death disappeared. How does this happen unless it's a cover-up?

“And now we discover Box 39, which contains a bunch of sealed files from her personal doctor, who I believe is responsible for her death.

“I firmly believe that the answers to what happened may be hidden in these files.”

Altringer, who runs the Ariel Investigations Agency in New Mexico, is campaigning to have Marilyn's death certificate changed from "probable suicide” to “murder” and has launched a Change.org petition, which so far has over 6,000 signatures.

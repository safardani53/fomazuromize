How to Pick the Right Pillows at Amazon

Filling Material 

When shopping for pillows at Amazon, Lehr says to first look at the filling material to determine the best option for you. Pillows come in all sorts of filling, including down, memory foam (both solid and shredded), cotton, and down alternative, which is typically made of polyester. When in doubt, Lehr recommends trying a down pillow, however, if it’s not in your price point, an alternative like the Amazon Basics Down-Alternative Pillow is a fantastic place to start.

Firmness and Support 

“It is important to select the right level of firmness when buying a pillow,” says Lehr. “Depending on the type of sleeper you are, the right firmness will ensure a good night’s sleep,” she adds.” Most Amazon descriptions share details on the firmness and support offered, however, it helps to have a baseline to go off of. If you’re a side sleeper, medium-firm might provide the best support. Medium-firm is also a safe choice for back sleepers. And, if you prefer to sleep on your stomach or end up on your stomach in your sleep, you might find that a soft- to medium-firm pillow fits your needs best. 

Read Reviews 

Since you can’t test them out before purchasing, Amazon reviews are one of the biggest assets when shopping on the Prime marketplace for pillows. Lehr recommends taking the time to read through the reviews on Amazon to help make your purchasing decision. 

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What brand makes the best pillows?

    If you’re shopping for the best pillow at Amazon, the Beckham Hotel Collection is an excellent brand to consider. We specifically love the Beckham Hotel Collection Bed Pillows because they are made from high-quality memory foam that offers a lot of support and has a luxury feel, despite the low-end price point. They are also machine washable, so they’re super easy to care for. For those looking for the best travel pillows, Ostrichpillow is a must-try, and we love the Ostrichpillow Go because it offers lots of ergonomic support, plus it packs down well, so it doesn’t take up a lot of room when not in use.

  • What type of pillow is best?

    “Down pillows are made with light feathers which offer incredible softness, comfort, sustainability, and last longer than pillows made with synthetic fibers,” says Lehr. With that in mind, down pillows — like the Continental Bedding Luxury Down Pillow — aren’t the only choice when it comes to high-quality pillows. If you want something with a little extra list, Lehr recommends cotton pillows because they “are filled with a firm, natural material that provides more density.” You can also opt for a down alternative like the budget-friendly Amazon Basics Down-Alternative Pillow which “uses a filling made of polyester and can be cleaned in the washing machine,” says Fehr. “Polyester pillows are a low price-point option, filled with polyester, and easy to clean.”

  • Are expensive pillows worth it?

    “Investing in a good night’s sleep could begin with the pillows you use,” says Lehr. And, while it is possible to find a fantastic budget-friendly pillow, Lehr explains that some pillows made with inexpensive materials are usually thin and lumpy, which could have an impact on sleep quality and overall comfort. “A high-quality pillow will offer comfort, support, a firmness option, and breathability which are all essential for a good night’s sleep,” she adds, noting how high-quality pillows tend to have a longer lifespan, too.

  • How often should you replace your pillows?

    When buying a pillow — either at Amazon or from anywhere — you want to ensure they will last. According to Jessica De Kler, an interior designer and associate at CetraRuddy, pillows (and protective covers) should be replaced every two to three years. “Signs that your pillow should be replaced include loss in shape, neck and shoulder pain, and skin irritation,” Lehr says.

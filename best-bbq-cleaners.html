Can I use oven cleaner on a BBQ?

It’s important to choose a cleaning product based on what your barbecue is made from.

Most cleaning products are fine to use on stainless steel for example, but some higher end barbecues might need specialist care because they have cast iron or enamel grills.

This is why many well-known brands produce their own cleaning products to care for their grills.

You can use your regular oven cleaner in some cases but bear in mind that they’re also quite harsh and abrasive and can damage materials like enamel.

So before you use it, always check the cleaning instructions from your barbecue maker as well as on the cleaning product.

    How to clean a BBQ

    Charcoal and gas barbecues have slightly different cleaning processes although the procedure to clean the grill is largely the same.

    Start with preparing your cleaning area by laying down some newspapers to protect the ground from any grease and dirt and put on some sturdy gloves.

    To clean the grill, brush off any stuck-on food and ash with a wire brush and wipe off any excessive grease using some scrunched up newspapers or old rags.

    Next, spray the grill with your chosen cleaner then set it aside and let it get to work - the instructions on the product should tell you how long - while you clean the rest of the barbecue.

    Once the cleaner has done its job, it should just be a case of rising down the grill, using a steel wood pad (or a similarly sturdy scrubber depending on the material your grill is made from) on any tough bits.

    You might also need to do a second rinse with mild soap and water depending on the cleaner you use.

    For higher end barbecues, there may be special cleaning instructions so make sure you check those as well.

    How to clean a charcoal BBQ

    Once you’ve allowed your barbecue to cool down and tipped out all the ash, it’s time to get to work cleaning it.

    Start with the base. You’ll need to brush out any remaining ash and debris from inside the base and then wash it with some mild soap and a steel wood pad.

    Depending on the material that’s covering the outside of the barbecue, it might just be a case of wiping it down with mild soap and a microfibre cloth and then rinsing with water.

    Once it’s clean, rinse off any remaining soap and then either air dry or wipe clean.

    Repeat the same process with the lid.

    How to clean a gas BBQ

    For gas barbecues, it’s generally recommended that you do a pre-clean while it’s still hot so it's easiest to do this as soon you’ve finished your barbecuing.

    You simply heat the barbecue on full with the lid down for 30 minutes, which should char any stuck-on food.

    Then with the gas off but the barbecue still warm (not hot), you brush off any debris using a wire brush. Make sure your gloves are waterproof and heat resistant for this.

    Once that’s done, disconnect the gas canister and allow everything to cool.

    You can take out all the removable parts from the barbecue to clean once the barbecue is fully cooled. Just follow the same instructions as for cleaning the grill.

    For the interior of the barbecue, brush out any debris with a sturdy brush - a wire brush might scratch the material so it’s best to check the guidance from your barbecue maker.

    Spray the interior and lid with your chosen cleaner, avoiding the burner tubes, and wait for the instructed time - then it should just be a case of wiping off the cleaning solution and allowing it to dry before reassembling it.

Miles Boykin Bio | Early Life, Education, & Parents

Miles Boykin was born and raised in Tinley Park, Illinois, to parents George Boykin, Jr. and Felicia Boykin. Moreover, Miles’s father works as a plant manager. Likewise, his mother is a nurse and also a teacher at Northwestern College.

Aside from his parents, Miles grew up with his older brother George Boykin. Notably, his brother was the first to play football in his family.

Likewise, he used to play football in a tight-end position during high school. 

But sadly, George suffered a severe head injury during his practice, which unfortunately led him to quit his football career. Following that injury, Miles made a way to get his older brother back onto the field in spirit. 

So, he thoughtfully changed his jersey number from 20 to his brother’s jersey number 81 to keep his pride alive in the field. Miles mentioned in one of his interviews saying,

“I just asked him, ‘Can I have 81 and wear it?’ And he said ‘Yeah’ without hesitation, so ever since then I’ve never had a different number,” Every time I step onto the field, I’m thinking of him, thinking of my family, so it just means a lot for me to wear that number.”

Moreover, Miles completed his high school education at Providence Catholic High School.

While there, Boykin was named Illinois Player of the Year by Champaign News-Gazette and Chicago Catholic League co-offensive MVP in the senior year.

On top of that, Miles also used to play basketball during his high school and was a two-time Chicago Catholic League all-conference selection in basketball.

After a successful high school career, Miles enrolled at the University of Notre Dame.

Miles Boykin |College Career and Stats

Just like his high school career, he also had a successful college career. Boylin was one of the best players in Notre Dame and was even named Notre Dame Offensive Player of the year in senior season.

Coming to his sophomore season, Miles had 12 receptions for 253 yards and two touchdowns.

Similarly, Miles was also named Citrus Bowl’s most valuable player (MVP) in the 2018 season for his outstanding performance. He recorded three receptions for 102 yards and the game-winning touchdown.

To sum up, he had 77 catches for 1,206 yards and 11 touchdowns over three seasons.

Miles had 59 catches for 872 yards and eight scores — all team-leading totals in the season that ended in the College Football Playoff.

After that season, Miles surprisingly decided to leave for the NFL before one season of eligibility.

IF you're a savvy saving shopper looking for a bargain, then a Very return pallet might just be the perfect purchase for you.

From how to buy a return pallet to the goods you could possibly get inside - here's everything you need to know.

What is a Very return pallet?

Return pallets are pallet loads of returned goods from wholesalers.

Usually, they are resold unseen to anyone who's willing to take a chance.

They could contain goods worth thousands which can be resold - making a pretty penny from your purchase.

However, you need to be ready to take a gamble as not all pallets pay off.

Pallets can consist of anything, from clothing, watches and jewellery through to electrical and household items.

Very sells clothes, homeware, garden ware, toys and electricals, so you could end up shirts and dresses or it could be stacked with iPads and camera lenses.

In 2023, a money-saving mum purchased two huge Very pallets with almost £2k worth of bargains including trampolines, lawnmowers & kids’ toys.

Similarly, a bargain hunter splashed £385 on a Very returns pallet full of toys, including a £130 Go Kart bike and a Lego set worth £229.

What other companies sell return pallets?

Most big retailers package off their returned goods into pallets to be resold.

John Lewis, Amazon, Argos, Currys and Screwfix are just some of the companies that sell off pallets with returned goods.

Similarly, you can purchase themed pallets including beauty returns and electrical goods.

However, it is important to understand the rules around returning a return pallet.

Many of the listings do not allow returns, and the pallets must be kept as is or resold by the buyer.

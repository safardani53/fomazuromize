Drue Chrisman | Football Career

High School Career

Drue Chrisman attended the La Salle High School, where he played quarterback and defense and was a punter. During his tenure, he competed in the Greater Catholic League.

However, his quarterback days were put to an end due to an injury, and thus, he shifted to being a punter.

Initially, he commenced as a punter in the YouTube videos and later moved to play alongside coach Dick Seitz. 

While as a high schooler, he posted an average of 46.4 and 43.0 yards per punt during the 2015 and 2014 seasons for LaSalle.

By the end of his high school, Chrisman stood as the No. 1 punter prospect in the 2016 recruiting class. 

Overall, both 247Sports and the prestigious Kohl’s kicking and punting organization ranked him as the top punter. Alongside it, the Scout ranked him second on the list.

Altogether, he ended his career as a quarterback with a 21-yard pass. It also had a fake punt in the second quarter of the Big Ten Championship Game vs. Wisconsin.

Collegiate Career and College Stats

Drue Chrisman enrolled in the Ohio State University following his high school days.

At first, he featured as the redshirt freshman to have five years on the campus and then pursue his Masters’s degree. 

As a freshman, he learned punting with punter Cameron Johnston.

Afterward, he stood as the semifinalist for the prestigious Ray Guy Award and earned Third Team All-Big Ten honors. He averaged a net of 41.6 yards per punt.  

Furthermore, he maintained an average of 44.3 yards and 45 yards per punt in the latter years. In 2018, Drue stood as the No. 1 ranking in the Big Ten in net punting while he posted 41.6 yards. 

The same year, he also had 29 punts which was the second-most in school history. At the end of his senior career, Drue became one of the best punters in the Big Ten and nationally.

Furthermore, he made one of his longest punts in a game against Michigan State. 

Likewise, he was fifth in Ohio State history with a career average of 43.9 yards per punt. According to his college stats, he made 73 punts through 3253 yards while averaging 44.6.

NFL Career

Despite his great abilities, the punter went undrafted in the 2021 NFL Draft. Nevertheless, in May, he signed a contract with the Cincinnati Bengals as an undrafted free agent.

However, the Bengals eventually waived him in October after releasing and re-signing him twice from May to October. After that, the Pittsburgh Steelers signed the Punter to their practice squad in December, only to release him two days later.

A few days later, the Bengals signed Chrisman to their practice squad again but released him on January 18, 2022. Finally, the player signed a reserve/future contract with the team in February.

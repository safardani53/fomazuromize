A thunderstorm junkie and a certified bookworm—Kate Bilo is an American meteorologist. She is best known for her current position as a chief meteorologist for CBS3.

Previously, she has served as a meteorologist for AccuWeather and Bloomberg Television Network. She also guest-hosted CBS’s Daytime Emmy Award-nominated talk show THE TALK.

Kate Bilo Holds Double Undergraduate Degree

The Phoenixville, Philadelphia native was born in 1986 and celebrated her birthday every year on June 20.

She holds a double undergraduate degree: a Bachelor of Science degree in Spanish and International Business from Penn State University and a Bachelor of Science degree in Geosciences from Mississippi State University.

After graduation from Penn State University, the aspiring meteorologist started her professional career in State College for AccuWeather in 2004. During her time at AccuWeather, she delivered forecasts on TV and radio for several AccuWeather clients, including FOX News, CNBC, ABC News Now, and many local stations.

After serving Accuweather for six years, she joined Bloomberg Television Network in 2010 as the main morning weather anchor.

Soon in October of 2010, she joined the CBS3 team as a chief meteorologist for the Eyewitness News weather team. She currently earns an estimated average salary of $77,592 as a chief meteorologist at CBS3.

Who Is Kate Bilo’s Husband?

Moving onto her love life, Kate has been happily married to her husband Scott Eby (a software engineer) since August 31, 2007. The couple celebrated their eleventh wedding anniversary in 2018, and not once after marriage have there been any rumors of divorce or affair between the lovers.

Not only is her love life full of bliss, but her family life is also blissful. She is a mother to her three beautiful kids Leo (2010), Anders (2012), and baby Solenne Marit Elisabeth (2017).

Another problematic situation arose after giving birth to her daughter Solenne in 2017. She pulled a muscle from picking up her newborn baby.

But when taking an Advil didn’t relieve the pain, she got hospitalized. Later the doctors found out that she suffered a pulmonary embolism (a condition where multiple arteries in the lungs get blocked by a blood clot).

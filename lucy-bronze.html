Early Life, Parents, & Education

Lucy Roberta Tough Bronze, well-known as Lucy Bronze, was born in Berwick-upon-Tweed, England. She is the second daughter of her parents, Joaquin Bronze and Kim Bronze.

Together, they raised Lucy and her two siblings, an elder brother named Jorge and a younger sister, Sophie.

Lucy’s father is of Portuguese descent, while her mother is pure English, which makes her British-Portuguese with white ethnic background.

Regarding her education, Lucy went to the Duchess Community High School located at Duchess, Alnwick, and later moved to the States.

She enrolled at UNC in North Carolina and later transferred to Leeds Metropolitan University.

In her early days, Bronze started playing soccer in her high school and represented the under-12 academy-level team, Sunderland. Later, after turning 16 in 2007, Lucy captained the under-16 squad.

From U-16 To The Manchester City

As mentioned above, Lucy has been playing in the field since high school. Already a captain for the under-16 team, she was named Manager’s Player of the Year after Sunderland finished third in the FA Women’s Premier League Northern Division. 

Likewise, the following season, they won the league, and the team gained promotion to the National Division. After that, she also appeared in the 2009 FA Women’s Cup final but they lost to Arsenal by 2-1.

Right after her shift to the states, Lucy began playing for the North Carolina Tar Heels women’s soccer team. Impressed by her, coach Anson Dorrance offered her a scholarship.

In December 2009, Bronze became the first British player to win an N.C.A.A. Cup, followed by All-American honors after scoring three goals and four assists in 24 games. 

In September 2010, Bronze signed with Everton and was named a part of the UEFA Women’s Champions League. But she left the team in 2012 and signed with the local rivals, Liverpool.

Apart from that, Lucy made another change by joining Manchester City in November 2014. Fierce from the beginning, she scored two goals in her debut game against 

Her goal helped the City qualify for the UEFA Women’s Championships League for the first time, making history.

Lucy and the Blues were proving to be formidable opponents on the field. More so, it was Bronze who helped the team win their second FA WSL Cup. It got her the FA WSL 1 Players’ Player of the Year award. 

Level Up With Barcelona

Lucy was part of the Olympique Lyonnais and signed a three-year contract in August 2017. She made eight appearances during the 2017-18 season and scored two goals.

Likewise, the star player also played in the final match of the UEFA Women’s Champions League and even helped the team to win the competition. Moreover, she continued scoring goals in almost all of her appearances.

Her contribution made Bronze the Team of the Year for D1 Feminine and Women’s Champions League Squad of the Season in 2018.

Besides that, Lucy was one of the players to be shortlisted for the inaugural Women’s Ballon d’Or award; and later among the ten players to be nominated for the UEFA Women’s Player of the Year Award. 

In the 2018-19 season, Lucy made 29 appearances for Lyon and scored two goals.

She collected her second Division 1 Feminine League title winner’s medal, Coupe de France Feminine, and three UEFA Women’s Champions League with Lyon.

On 8 September 2020, Bronze returned to Manchester City on two years deal and at the end of 2020 was placed third on the 100 best lists produced by The Guardian.

Before the start of the 2021- 22 season, she had knee surgery due to which she could not perform in 2021.

In June 2022, Bronze agreed to join Barcelona on a two-year contract after the expiration of her contract with Manchester.

She made her debut for Barcelona’s first league game against UDG Tenerife in a 2-0 win; she moved into the midfield in the 60th minute to make plays with England teammate Keira Walsh.

Bronzo scored her first goal with Barcelona against Atletico Madrid on 27 November 2022 in the Otro Clasico match.

England’s National Team

In March 2007, Lucy became a part of the England under-17 squad and came fourth in the FIFA U-17 Women’s World Cup held in New Zealand.

She continued to represent England in 2009 and 2010 by joining their under-20 training camp.

On June 26, 2013, Lucy made her official debut for the England senior team against Japan. The following year, she scored her first goal against Belarus in World Cup qualifiers.

Later, Bronze joined the English Women’s soccer team at the 2015 World Cup.

Lucy was widely praised for her performance, and her name was included in the 2015 FIFA Women’s World Cup All-Star and shortlisted for the Golden Ball.

Furthermore, she played in England’s national squad in UEFA Women’s Euro 2017 (lost in the semi-finals to the Netherlands) and the 2019 World Cup, where England finished fourth.

In the 2022 UEFA Women’s Euro final against Germany, she assisted in a goal that made England victorious. Lucy passed the ball from a corner touch into the box which Chloe Kelly converted into a goal with 10 minutes left in the extra time.
